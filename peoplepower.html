PEOPLE POWER

In human daily lives, people always search for love, companion … In general, we can assume that human needs communication. With the development of the internet, new way to communicate were invented and developed so fast in the start of 2000s. This new way of communication has changed the internet and social media from the web change the people to people change the web.

The start of this revolution started out with 2 start-ups that shaped the road for the new social media and social communication: Digg and Youtube. In the old time, in order to get on TV, each host had a crew and travelled around to film and pushed the video to the TV. This process costed the huge amount of money and average people couldn’t do things like that. But with Youtube, people could film and broadcast their video on Youtube. In the same page for news, in the old time, news was only published by editors and news organizer like New York Times. However, with Digg, people could share and public news freely and Digg’s system let people to vote for the news. If the news had more vote it would be pinned permanently.

Along with the development of Digg and Youtube another technology was developed that is the .MP3. The .mp3 file compress the audio files into a small and effective version which is easy to move around the internet. Therefore, users could upload, store, play and download music from the internet. By this development, more people sought out for music and download it regardless the copyright. But it was hard and time consuming to find music. One software was created in order for people to download music directly from the collection of other people. The technology behind this software is peer to peer connection and this software’s name is Napster. Napster was created by Shawn Fanning, which let people to have their own collection and download music directly from other people’s collection which place in other PC. In 6/1999, there were 10000 people download music using Napster per week which empty the old school music shop. However, Napster did not consider about the copyright of music. Consequently, it started out a controversial problem between Napster and music producing company. Many music record companies started to question about the copyright, also the music artist such as Metalika triggered a discussion in the community about this problem. Metalika wanted Napster to ban all the users who had download their music from Napster. In May 2000, RIAA sued Napster and Napster had to shut down. This story inspired companies such as Apple to create Itunes Music Store, where people have to pay for songs and maintain the copyright of the songs.

In the same manner, Youtube users uploaded recorded famous TV shows to Youtube which also create the same controversial problem. Youtube was sued as well, but not alike with Napster, Youtube had a big sugar daddy – Google – supported whichs helped Youtube to stand strong and survive the sue. After that Youtube has tried so hard to maintain the copyright until these days.

In the stories of above companies, we can see that not only backend editors create the content but also now can create and publish content. These changes created Web 2.0, where now people are the one who create the contents. Two of the most innovative pioneers in Web 2.0 are My Space and Facebook. My space created a social media website which gave the users a webpage that can be customized by the will of the users. Supported by Murphy, Myspace became a biggest social media network in 2002. With the same ambition, Mark Zuckerberg created Facebook which mapped all the relationships that people can have and generated the social graph. Facebook also create a platform for users can create pages to share meaningful information. This leaded to a new type of advertising as well.
